Use Case 01

🏋️ Gym / Fitness Studio Management

Most local gyms track memberships on a notebook — renewals are missed and revenue walks out the door silently.

Problem Statement

Local gyms and fitness studios operate manually — member data is kept in registers, fee collection tracked in Excel or paper, and attendance noted in a physical book.

With no automated alerts, membership renewals go unnoticed until the member simply stops coming. Staff manually remind members via WhatsApp when they remember.

The result: avoidable revenue loss, no data to track retention, and zero visibility into peak hours or most-used equipment.

Target Users

Primary: Gym Owner / Manager — oversees membership, fees, and staff.

Secondary: Front Desk Staff — handles check-in, fee collection, and enquiries.

End Users: Members — track their own attendance, plan, and renewal date.

Key Features (Minimum 5)
  • Member registration with plan, fee, and photo
  • Biometric / QR attendance check-in system
  • Auto membership renewal alerts (7 days / 1 day before)
  • Diet & workout plan assignment per member
  • Daily / monthly revenue reports with charts
  • Enquiry pipeline — leads, trials, conversions
  • Staff salary and attendance tracking
Business Value & Why They'd Invest
Renewal reminders alone can recover 15–25% of lapsed members per month
Digital attendance removes front-desk bottleneck at peak hours
Revenue dashboards let owners spot slow months early and run targeted offers
Member portal increases perceived value — members stay loyal to gyms with apps
ROI visible within 1 month — even 5 member retentions cover software cost

Use Case 02

🔧 Car / Bike Service Center

Service centers maintain job cards and parts inventory on paper registers — customer history is lost between visits.

Problem Statement

Car and bike service centers in India primarily use paper-based job cards. When a vehicle returns for the same issue, mechanics have no visibility into past repairs, parts used, or warranty status.

Parts inventory is tracked manually — resulting in over-ordering, parts running out mid-job, and billing errors where parts are forgotten or double-charged.

Customers receive no service reminders (oil change due, free check-up after service), which means repeat business depends entirely on the customer remembering to return.

Target Users

Primary: Workshop Owner / Service Manager — oversees job cards, billing, and parts.

Secondary: Mechanic / Technician — updates job status and parts used per vehicle.

End Users: Customers — receive status updates, estimates, and service reminders.

Key Features (Minimum 5)
  • Digital job card creation with vehicle details and complaint
  • Complete vehicle service history by registration number
  • Parts inventory with low-stock alerts and auto-reorder suggestions
  • Estimate sharing with customer approval before work starts
  • Real-time job status updates (Received → In Progress → Ready)
  • Automated service due reminders via SMS / WhatsApp
  • Final bill generation with parts and labor breakdown
Business Value & Why They'd Invest
Service reminders bring back 30–40% of one-time customers as repeat visitors
Digital estimates eliminate disputes — customer approves before work begins
Parts inventory prevents mid-job delays and reduces over-purchasing by 20%
Service history builds customer trust — "you serviced my car 3 times" is powerful
Faster billing at delivery reduces customer wait time and frees counter staff

Use Case 03

💊 Pharmacy / Medical Store Software

Small pharmacies track stock and expiry dates manually — expired medicines reach customers and stock-outs cause daily revenue loss.

Problem Statement

Local pharmacies in India — especially non-chain, independent stores — manage inventory on paper or basic Excel. Expiry dates of hundreds of medicine batches are never tracked systematically, creating real health and legal risk.

Stock-outs happen without warning — popular medicines run out, the owner realizes only when a customer asks, and the sale is lost. No purchasing pattern data means buying decisions are guesswork.

Billing is slow, GST calculation is manual, and there's no customer prescription history to identify repeat buyers or flag doctor-specific prescriptions.

Target Users

Primary: Pharmacy Owner / Pharmacist — manages stock, billing, purchasing, and compliance.

Secondary: Counter Staff — handles billing and stock updates during the day.

End Users: Customers — optional loyalty card / prescription history portal.

Key Features (Minimum 5)
  • Medicine stock management with batch and expiry tracking
  • Expiry alerts — 30, 15, and 7 days before batch expiry
  • Low stock alerts with auto purchase order generation
  • Fast billing with barcode scan — GST auto-applied by medicine category
  • Prescription record with doctor name and patient details
  • Supplier management and purchase history tracking
  • Daily / monthly sales report with most-sold medicines dashboard
Business Value & Why They'd Invest
Expiry tracking prevents legal penalties — selling expired medicine is a criminal offence under Drugs Act
Low-stock alerts eliminate lost sales — every stock-out is a direct revenue loss
Faster billing reduces customer wait time — critical for busy morning rush
GST auto-calculation removes filing errors and CA dependency for monthly returns
Purchase history reveals top-selling medicines — smarter bulk buying at better margins
